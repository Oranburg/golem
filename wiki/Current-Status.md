# Current Status

## Live
- The website at oranburg.law/golem/, mobile-first, in the Oranburg house style with the light and dark toggle and the breadcrumb back to the main site.
- The real systems are now split across three pages, three faces of one question:
  - **Field Guide (the battlefield):** 39 devices with photos, in two valence sections. **Life-destroying** holds 31 weapons, counter-systems, and sensors (offensive strike, defensive point-defense, barriers and the sensing that cues them). **Life-saving** holds 8 battlefield-medicine devices (the BEAR extraction robot, the S-MET mule, the Zipline medical drone, the SAVe field ventilator, the Mark I NAAK auto-injector, the ER-REBOA catheter, the ZOLL AutoPulse, and mobile ECMO).
  - **Civilian Devices:** 21 off-battlefield autonomous machines, from the CyberKnife, the AED, the ICD, the artificial-pancreas insulin system, and the STAR surgical robot to the da Vinci, self-driving cars, sidewalk-delivery robots, an autonomous tractor, and household machines. Some make irreversible calls in hospitals, cars, and bodies; others only run themselves.
  - **IP & Services:** a sortable registry of 27 intangible systems (military targeting software, predictive policing, COMPAS, organ allocation, Stuxnet and NotPetya, agentic AI, algorithmic trading, smart contracts and DAOs, content moderation, and the welfare and scoring algorithms), the golems with no body to photograph.
- Each card states what the device is and, where a source supports it, its human-control posture; the interpretive analysis was removed from the cards. The four-flag principle-sorter carries the classification, set from the research bench where it covers a system and audited by hand otherwise. The field guide and civilian pages are photo grids; the registry is a sortable table.
