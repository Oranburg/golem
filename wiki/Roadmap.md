# Roadmap

The project moves from a single published essay to a research program with a public home.

## Phase 0, done
The Public Discourse essay, *AI, Magnifica Humanitas, and the Law of the Golem*, published June 11, 2026.

## Phase 1, done
The research apparatus: a verified Jewish-law study (agency, the fire-arrow chain, the directness requirement, the residue of liability before Heaven), a bench of thirty real automated systems with sourcing, and an image library of those systems with credits and rights tracked in a JSON catalog.

## Phase 2, done in draft
Three articles share the argument at three registers: the law-review essay (illustrated), the popular magazine version, and the field guide. All are drafts carrying open verification flags.

## Phase 3, done
A public website: static, deployed to GitHub Pages at oranburg.law/golem/, generated from the project content by a single build script.

## Phase 4, next: the analytical layer
The field guide sorts the machines with four flags borrowed from the AI-policy debate (lethal, irreversible, the machine makes the call, no answerable human). The project's own theory of responsibility was never brought to the classification. This phase supplies it:
- A two-axis map of the systems, gravity of the harm against distance from a reachable, controlling human, replacing the four parallel flags with one picture (#11).
- A short "why a ban, not a liability rule" page, grounded in Calabresi-Melamed, the bridge from the accountability gap to the prohibition, now that the 1972 article is in LawJ (#12).
- The first diagram reworked to fork between property-rule and liability-rule protection, instead of beginning at "harm," which silently assumes the liability frame.
- A sidebar in the essay at the hinge from diagnosing the gap to defending the ban.

The open fork that gates the map: are the two axes gravity and distance-from-a-reachable-human, with the machine's discretion and the reachable-person test folded into the second, or is discretion a third axis.

## Phase 5: finish and place
Clear the open verification flags (see [[Current Status]] and Issues), run the full style and citation passes on the articles and the new theory prose, settle the title slate (including a new essay title and a clearer label than "Magazine"), and clear image rights or confirm fair use for each manufacturer image. Then place the articles with their venues.

## Phase 6, optional
Non-gating enhancements, tracked in Issues. Astro lives here, gated on one feature: an interactive version of the two-axis map (filter by domain, hover for a system's flags and posture, toggle the regulator's line). Build the map static first; migrate to Astro only if it wants to be an island. Also [[Future Project: Download Your Insights]].
